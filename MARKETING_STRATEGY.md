## 1. Brand positioning & one-liner

### The brand idea

Alliances PRO is **a CRM platform, not just a CRM product**. One workspace, one login, one billing relationship — but the customer can switch between vertical-specific CRMs the moment their business grows into a new line. Sales today, Education tomorrow, Real Estate or Healthcare next year.

That platform-vs-product framing is the wedge. Almost every competitor sells you a generic CRM that *also* tries to fit into specialty workflows. Alliances PRO sells you a specialty CRM that happens to share a platform with other specialty CRMs.

### One-liner (for hero, ads, meta description)

> **The CRM platform built for service businesses that grow sideways.**
> One login. One inbox. Sales CRM today, Education CRM tomorrow, more verticals as you scale.

### Alternate one-liners (A/B test these in ads)

- "The only CRM that grows with your business model — not just your headcount."
- "Stop forcing your service business into a generic CRM. Pick the one built for your industry."
- "Multi-vertical CRM for agencies, sales teams, educators, and the next thing you start."

### Brand voice

- **Confident, not arrogant.** "We built this because the alternatives were wrong" — not "we're the best."
- **Practical over poetic.** Specifics beat adjectives. Say "drag-and-drop pipeline with 7 default stages" not "powerful pipeline management."
- **Operator-to-operator.** Talk like one founder talking to another. No corporate fluff.
- **Anti-bloat.** Pride in being focused. "We don't do everything — we do your thing well."

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

You confirmed three ICPs. Here's how to think about each — including which to lead with on the homepage.

### ICP 1 — Shopify agencies & e-commerce service providers (PRIMARY at launch)

**Who they are:** 2-30 person Shopify Plus agencies, app developers, e-commerce consultants, fractional CMO/CRO shops. They juggle 10-50 active client accounts, sell retainers + one-off projects, and need to track Shopify-specific signals (which apps the prospect uses, GMV tier, theme vendor, etc.).

**Pain:**
- HubSpot/Pipedrive don't understand Shopify context — agencies maintain spreadsheets alongside their CRM.
- They lose hours a week prospecting in Shopify app stores, then re-typing data into their CRM.
- Generic CRMs charge per-seat at agency-scale ($800+/mo for 10 seats on HubSpot Pro).

**Why Alliances PRO wins:** Shopify-aware lead capture (store URL, GMV bracket, app stack), built-in agency project tracking alongside the deal, agency-friendly pricing.

**Where they hang out:** Shopify Partners community, /r/shopify, Twitter/X (#shopifydev), Indie Hackers, Shopify Unite, Reconvert / Klaviyo / Recharge ecosystems.

### ICP 2 — SMB sales teams (5-50 reps, general)

**Who they are:** B2B sales teams in services, software, and light manufacturing. Owner-operator or VP of Sales runs the buying decision. They've outgrown spreadsheets and free HubSpot, but Salesforce is overkill and Pipedrive feels generic.

**Pain:**
- Pipedrive is "fine" but doesn't have email campaigns or marketing automation built in — they end up paying for Mailchimp + Pipedrive + Calendly + a dialer.
- HubSpot starts cheap and balloons to $4K/mo by year two.
- Existing tools weren't built around how their specific industry sells.

**Why Alliances PRO wins:** Honest, predictable pricing. Lead → Deal → Project → Invoice in one tool. Email campaigns, call logs, and tasks all built-in (no add-ons).

**Where they hang out:** LinkedIn, sales subreddits (/r/sales, /r/sweatystartup), G2/Capterra reviews, sales podcasts.

### ICP 3 — Freelancers & solopreneurs

**Who they are:** Consultants, freelance designers/devs, fractional execs, 1-3 person agencies. They want a free or near-free starting tier and they need the CRM to *also* be their project tracker and invoice source.

**Pain:**
- HubSpot Free is great until it isn't — they hit limits at exactly the wrong moment.
- Notion + Google Sheets + Calendly is a duct-tape stack that breaks at 20 clients.

**Why Alliances PRO wins:** A real 14-day trial with no credit card, then $19/mo Pro (10 users included — no penalty as you hire your first employee), and the same tool keeps working when you scale.

**Where they hang out:** Indie Hackers, X/Twitter, freelance subreddits, Productized.com, Justin Welsh's audience.

### ICP prioritization for the launch site

| Position | ICP | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero leads with** | SMB sales teams (general) | Largest TAM, broadest pull from organic search |
| **Secondary callout** | Shopify agencies | Differentiator + your strongest existing fit |
| **Tertiary** | Freelancers | Low entry price ($19) + future-upsell story |
| **Coming soon** | Education CRM | Build the waitlist, hint at the platform vision |

## 3. Messaging pillars & value propositions

Five pillars. Every page, ad, and email maps back to one of these.

### Pillar 1 — One platform, every vertical

**Headline:** "One login. Every CRM you'll ever need."
**Proof:** Sales CRM live. Education CRM in beta. Architecture supports unlimited vertical add-ons.
**Why it matters:** Customers don't have to switch tools when their business pivots or expands.

### Pillar 2 — Built for how you actually sell

**Headline:** "A CRM that knows what 'pipeline' means in your industry."
**Proof:** Vertical-specific objects (Shopify store, GMV, app stack — for Sales; programs, applicants, enrollments — for Education).
**Why it matters:** Generic CRMs make you bend your process to the tool. Alliances PRO bends to you.

### Pillar 3 — Lead → Deal → Delivery in one tool

**Headline:** "Stop paying for five tools to manage one client."
**Proof:** Leads, Deals, Projects, Tasks, Calls, Email Campaigns, Contact Lists — all in one workspace, all included.
**Why it matters:** Cuts SaaS spend by 60-80% vs. HubSpot + Mailchimp + Calendly + Asana + Aircall stack.

### Pillar 4 — Honest, transparent pricing

**Headline:** "No 'starts at' games. The price you see is the price you pay."
**Proof:** $19/mo Pro or $39/mo Business — flat per workspace, never per seat. 14-day free trial, no credit card. No mandatory onboarding fees. No per-feature unlocks.
**Why it matters:** Trust is the #1 reason small teams churn off HubSpot — they feel surprised by the bill.

### Pillar 5 — Your data, your team, your control

**Headline:** "Workspace-level isolation. Member roles. Per-workspace SMTP. Export anytime."
**Proof:** Multi-tenant arch with row-level isolation, granular role permissions, configurable email per workspace, SOC2 roadmap.
**Why it matters:** Agencies and small teams worry about who sees what. Make this concrete.

### 4.2 Deep dive — competitor by competitor

#### HubSpot

- **Where they win:** Brand. Free tier with marketing+sales. Massive content/SEO moat.
- **Where they lose:** The "starts at $20" turns into $800-4,000/mo by year two. Marketing Hub Professional alone is $890/mo. Onboarding fees of $1,500-3,000 on Pro+ plans. Limits on contacts inflate the bill.
- **Customer complaints (pulled from G2/Reddit):** "Bait and switch on pricing." "Onboarding fee is non-negotiable." "Required to bundle Sales + Marketing Hub at higher tiers."
- **The wedge for Alliances PRO:** Honest, flat pricing. Email campaigns and contact lists *included* — not a separate Hub. No mandatory onboarding fee.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs HubSpot — same features, 70% less."

#### Pipedrive

- **Where they win:** Pipeline UX is iconic. Simple. Sales-focused.
- **Where they lose:** No native marketing automation. Reports are weak below the Power tier. Email + Calendly + automation come as $13-$32/mo add-ons. Per-seat math hurts at 10+ users.
- **Customer complaints:** "Have to bolt on Mailchimp + a dialer + Calendly to get a full stack." "Pricing tiers feel arbitrary."
- **The wedge:** Same pipeline simplicity, but campaigns + calls + tasks built in. One bill.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Pipedrive — pipeline you love, marketing you don't have to bolt on."

#### Zoho CRM

- **Where they win:** Cheap. Vast suite (45+ apps). Strong in India / APAC.
- **Where they lose:** UX feels like 2014. Each Zoho app is a separate sub-product with its own learning curve. Customer support lottery.
- **Customer complaints:** "Looks dated." "Constant upsell prompts." "Apps don't talk to each other as well as marketed."
- **The wedge:** Modern, opinionated UX. One product feel — not 45 sub-apps you have to glue together.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Zoho — modern UX, same price point."

#### Salesforce Starter / Pro

- **Where they win:** Brand. Enterprise legitimacy. Massive partner ecosystem.
- **Where they lose:** SMB-hostile. "Starter" still requires a consultant to set up. Per-seat pricing escalates brutally. Slow.
- **Customer complaints:** Universally cited as the #1 reason teams *want* to switch.
- **The wedge:** SMB-native. Live in 10 minutes. No consultant required.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Why teams switch from Salesforce to Alliances PRO."

#### Close

- **Where they win:** Built-in calling + email sequences. Power-user sales SDR stack.
- **Where they lose:** $49/seat starting. No real marketing layer. Niche to inside sales.
- **The wedge:** Same call logging + email sequences, fraction of the per-seat cost, broader feature set.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Close — sales velocity at half the seat cost."

#### Monday CRM

- **Where they win:** Visual, fun, brand-savvy.
- **Where they lose:** Shallow CRM depth (it's monday.com with a CRM template). "Item-based" billing is confusing. Reports weak.
- **The wedge:** Real CRM under the hood, not a project tool wearing a CRM costume.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Monday CRM — designed as a CRM, not a CRM-flavored work app."

#### Freshsales

- **Where they win:** AI scoring (Freddy). Fair pricing. Good support.
- **Where they lose:** Smaller brand. Fewer integrations.
- **The wedge:** Comparable feature/price, plus a multi-vertical platform story they can't tell.
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Freshsales — same value, plus a platform that grows with you."

#### Copper

- **Where they win:** Deeply Google Workspace integrated.
- **Where they lose:** Locked to Google. Price-deceptive ("Basic" tier has hard caps).
- **The wedge:** Works with anything (Google, Outlook, custom SMTP per workspace).
- **Comparison page angle:** "Alliances PRO vs Copper — Google integration without the lock-in."

## 5. Differentiation strategy

### Your four structural moats

1. **Per-workspace pricing, not per-seat.** Every direct competitor charges per user per month. You charge per workspace, flat. At any team size of 4+ users, you're cheaper than Pipedrive, HubSpot Starter, Zoho Std, Monday CRM Pro, and Close. At 10 users, you're roughly 5–10× cheaper. This is a pricing-architecture moat competitors can't match without alienating their existing customer base.
2. **Multi-vertical platform.** No competitor in your tier offers Sales + Education + roadmap verticals under one workspace + one bill. Features can be copied; an architecture choice can't be without a rewrite.
3. **Vertical-aware data models.** Shopify-aware lead capture for sales agencies. Program/applicant/enrollment objects for education. Generic CRMs would have to bolt these on as custom fields — yours are first-class.
4. **Lead → Deal → Project in one tool.** Most CRMs stop at the deal close. Alliances PRO follows the customer through delivery — which is exactly where service businesses live or die.

### Your two messaging moats

1. **Honest pricing.** Lean on this hard — it's the #1 churn driver from HubSpot, and the easiest "vs" story to win.
2. **Operator-built.** You're a founder building this for founders. Don't hide that. The "we built this because every alternative made us miserable" story is your origin story.

## 6. Pricing strategy & packaging

### Current tiers (from `backend/database/seeders/PlanSeeder.php`)

Two paid plans, both billed via LemonSqueezy. No persistent free tier — the on-ramp is a 14-day free trial (no credit card required, per `AuthController.php`).

| Plan | Monthly | Yearly | Yearly savings | Projects | Organizations | Leads | Members | Tasks | Trial | Featured |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pro** | **$19/mo** | **$190/yr** | $38 (≈ 2 months free) | 10 | 500 | 1,000 | 10 | Unlimited | 14 days | — |
| **Business** | **$39/mo** | **$390/yr** | $78 (≈ 2 months free) | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited | 14 days | ⭐ Recommended |

### Why this pricing is your sharpest weapon

- **Flat pricing per workspace beats per-seat math at any team size of 4+.** Pipedrive's cheapest tier is $14/seat × 10 users = $140/mo. Your Pro is $19/mo flat for 10 users. You're 7× cheaper for the same team.
- **Business at $39/mo for unlimited everything** is the most aggressive flat-fee CRM price in the market by a wide margin. Most competitors have no equivalent SKU at all.
- **No free tier** keeps your unit economics clean (Pipedrive doesn't have one either, for the same reason). The 14-day no-card trial gives you the trial-conversion funnel without the support cost of free-forever users.

### Recommendations

1. **Lead every CTA with "Start 14-day free trial — no credit card."** This is your single highest-impact conversion lever and your code already supports it (`trial_days = 14`).
2. **Make Business the default visual choice on the pricing page.** It's already `is_featured = true` in the seeder — honor that with a "Most popular" badge, slightly larger card, and primary-color CTA. The mental anchor is "$39 unlimited" not "$19 limited."
3. **Frame annual savings as "2 months free,"** not "save $38" — emotional framing outperforms percentage / dollar-amount framing. Your seeder already gives you exactly 2 months free on both plans.
4. **Side-by-side comparison column** on the pricing page: show "$39/mo Business" next to "$340/mo Pipedrive Advanced for 10 users." Make the math visible.
5. **Add a "Per-vertical add-on" line** (greyed out, "coming soon") for Education CRM (e.g., "+ Education CRM module — $29/mo per workspace"). Anchors the platform story and primes future revenue.
6. **Hide the Enterprise tier** behind "Talk to sales" — don't list it, but mention it. Protects margin, signals legitimacy.
7. **Fill in the LemonSqueezy variant IDs** in the seeder before launch (currently empty strings). Without these, checkout will fail.
8. **Optional: add a one-time "lifetime deal" SKU on AppSumo or Pitchground** for the launch month. Many SMB CRMs (NetHunt, Salesflare, Salesmate) used this to seed the first 1,000 customers. Price it at $99-149 lifetime for Pro tier.

### Pricing page principle

Show comparison against HubSpot, Pipedrive, and Monday CRM directly on the pricing page. A small green tag below each tier ("$X/mo less than [competitor] for the same features") converts. The dollar gap is so large that the comparison sells itself.

## 11. Conversion strategy

### CTAs

- **Primary CTA everywhere:** "Start 14-day free trial — no credit card"
- **Secondary CTA on heavy pages:** "Book a 15-min demo"
- **Tertiary on industry pages:** "See the [industry] workflow"

### Forms

- Use as few fields as you can: signup = email + password (or Google OAuth one-click).
- Contact form: Name, work email, company, team size, message. (5 fields max.)
- Demo form: Same as contact + preferred time (Calendly embed).
- All forms: server-side validation, honeypot anti-spam, reCAPTCHA v3 on contact + demo only.

### Social proof tiers

1. **Logos** in hero strip (immediate)
2. **Number** ("1,200+ teams") in hero (immediate)
3. **Testimonials** below features (mid-funnel)
4. **G2/Capterra badges** in footer (later, when you have them)
5. **Case studies** as full pages (most powerful, slowest to build — start collecting now)

### Friction-removers

- Live chat (Crisp or Intercom) on pricing page only — your highest-intent page
- Cookieless analytics (Plausible) — privacy-friendly, faster than GA, doesn't need a cookie banner
- Page-level CTAs every 1-1.5 screens

## 14. Open questions for you

These will sharpen the website copy. Answer them in any order:

1. **Brand color palette.** What's the primary brand color? The cosmic theme defaults to a warm beige tone — do you want to override?
2. **Founder story.** Are you OK leading with a personal "founder built this because..." story on the about page? It's high-conversion for ICP 1 and ICP 3.
3. **Existing customers.** How many real paying customers do you have today? This determines whether you put numbers in the hero or wait until you can.
4. **Education CRM timing.** When does the Education beta open to a waitlist? This determines how prominently to feature it.
5. **Geographic positioning.** You picked global English. Do you want to mention any specific region (e.g., "Built in Bangladesh, used worldwide") for credibility/transparency?
6. **Integrations.** Which integrations are live or near-live? This deserves a "Sponsors / Integrations" strip if you have ≥6.
7. **Demo readiness.** Can you offer a same-week demo today? If yes, "Book a demo" becomes a strong secondary CTA. If no, lead only with self-serve.
